  scope{what_we_observe|what_we_do_not}:
    what_we_observe: Whether structured knowledge is assimilated by LLMs| how that knowledge is used| and whether its usage becomes stable over time. The object of observation is not the model itself but the relationship between a knowledge domain and language models.
    what_we_do_not: We do not evaluate LLM performance| intelligence| or answer quality. We do not assign scores to models. We do not rank models as better or worse.

  instruments[5]{name|acronym|axis|measures|question|what_it_is_not}:
    LLM Adoption Readiness| LAR| Assimilation| Recognition of domain-specific concepts| recall across controlled prompt variations| emergence of consistent usage patterns| adoption thresholds over time.| Is this domain actually present in the model's behavior?| NOT answer correctness. NOT benchmark scoring. NOT model comparison.
    Contextual Assimilation Measurement Score| CAMS| Assimilation (depth)| Degree to which a LLM has integrated a structured concept into its semantic space| making it citable during reasoning.| Has the model internalized this knowledge deeply enough to cite it?| NOT a SEO score. NOT a sentiment score. NOT a traffic metric.
    Domain Semantic Pressure| DSP| Behavior| Vocabulary usage patterns| semantic pressure and overfitting signals| distortion or flattening of concepts| stability of references under prompt variation.| How is the knowledge being used once it exists?| NOT semantic similarity scoring. NOT linguistic quality assessment.
    Cognitive Routing Stability| CRS| Stability| Persistence of conceptual routing| drift velocity and stabilization time| convergence or decay of usage patterns| resistance to regression across model updates.| Does this knowledge persist| stabilize| or drift over time?| NOT temporal accuracy. NOT dataset freshness evaluation.
    Cognitive Routing Decay| CRD| Stability (decay)| Rate of knowledge degradation over time| regression velocity after model updates| erosion of previously stable conceptual routes| decay patterns across retraining cycles.| Is previously assimilated knowledge being lost or degraded?| NOT model versioning tracking. NOT changelog analysis. NOT performance benchmarking.

  measurement_principles{longitudinal|declared_beacon|transparency|slowness}:
    longitudinal: All instruments are time-aware. Single observations are not meaningful. Only repeated measurements over time allow detection of stabilization| identification of drift| and observation of adoption phases. Kat3x does not expose instantaneous scores as standalone evaluations.
    declared_beacon: Observation is declared| not hidden. No cloaking policy. Human interaction intentionally minimal. Explicit non-intervention policy to prevent contamination.
    transparency: Transparency of purpose| transparency of principles| transparency of observable outputs. But NOT transparency of sensitive techniques — to preserve the scientific integrity of observation and prevent gaming.
    slowness: Kat3x prioritizes long historical series| signal stability| noise reduction| and repeated observation. In a context dominated by instantaneous metrics| slowness is a methodological choice| not a limitation.

  relationship_with_chkcd{separation|roles}:
    separation: Ontological separation is strict. Kat3x instruments are designed to be interoperable with the CHKCD standard but independent from it.
    roles[2]:
      CHKCD defines structured knowledge artifacts (the observed object)
      Kat3x observes how such artifacts are assimilated and used (the observer sensor)

  non_goals[4]:
    Kat3x instruments are NOT optimization tools
    NOT prompt engineering utilities
    NOT training or fine-tuning frameworks
    NOT ranking or leaderboard systems — their purpose is observation| not intervention
